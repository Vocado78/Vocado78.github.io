About me

Hello, my name is Ingrid and I'm a front-end developer based in Vancouver, BC.

In September of 2016 I followed some friends' advice to try out some front-end coding in my spare time. I loved it! The problem-solving mindset, the creativity and attention to detail it requires, and the rapid progress within the technology industry really suited me. It turned out to be such a perfect match that I left a stable job in contracts management at a pharmaceutical company in April of 2017, to learn and practise front-end development full-time.

Some months later, I was excited landing my first developer job when I was hired as a JavaScript Engineer at an online casino company. With so many different types of technologies in their stack and new concepts such as containerisation with Docker and Kubernetes, it was overwhelming in the beginning to say the least. Curiosity and determination kept me going, making each of the pieces fall into place bit by bit, and making me a contributing member of the team.

However, as adventurous outdoor enthusiasts, my partner and I had considered moving abroad to the mountains for quite a while. Eventually we found a winner in Vancouver, Canada, and in June of 2019 we left Stockholm and Sweden behind to begin a new chapter in our lives. I look forward to getting to know Vancouver and taking another step towards becoming the best possible software developer I can be.

Are you looking to hire? Download my resume or send me a message! I'd love to see how I can contribute.

When I'm not coding I'm likely out exploring the city and its surroundings with my partner, solving crossword puzzles, out running, meeting friends or catching up with sport news.

Skills

Front-end technologies

I regularly work with the latest features of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and React.js. When writing unit tests I mainly use Jest, Enzyme, and Sinon. Other technologies that I am familiar with are Redux and GraphQL.

Back-end technologies

I also have experience in developing and maintaining Node.js microservices. Either in combination with Restify and CouchDB, or with Express.js and MongDB. For unit testing in Node.js, Mocha and Chai are my standard.

User-centricity

As user perception and satisfaction are a constant focus in my work, I aim to build easy-to-use, fast and functional web apps. Navigation and features should be intuitive and clear to the user right away.

Structure

Based on user stories, I like to have an overview of a project in progress that anyone could follow. I seek to write organised, clean code and document projects just enough to facilitate maintenance by other developers not initially involved.
